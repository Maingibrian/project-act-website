⬤ Field Journal

The Coalition
Takes the Shore.

Kuruwitu Beach, a locally‑managed marine area in Vipingo, faced an acute pollution crisis driven by the East African Coastal Current. 78 participants — corporate staff, KWS officers, and CBO members — assembled at dawn to confront it.

The operation pivoted from 20 small teams to 6 high‑density strike teams of 10, each dynamically deployed to trash hotspots. Team leaders surveyed the 300m stretch and eliminated wasted effort on clean sand. 18 participants served as judges, safety marshals, and hydration logisticians — keeping the operation safe, coordinated, and fully accountable.
