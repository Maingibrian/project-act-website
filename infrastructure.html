From Factory Floor to Shoreline

55 Bins.
Zero Budget.

True corporate social responsibility leverages existing industrial capacity. Rather than purchasing mass-produced plastic bins — which would themselves become future waste — we intercepted heavy-duty metal drums from the Revital Healthcare factory floor: drums originally used for medical-grade raw material storage. In total, 55 bins have been deployed across the Kilifi coastline through three distinct phases of installation.

These units were fully sanitized, welded shut at base seams, structurally reinforced with coastal corrosion in mind, and fitted with high-visibility livery by our internal engineering team. Every hour of labor, every weld, and every coat of paint was absorbed within Revital's existing operational budget — making this one of the most cost-effective waste infrastructure deployments on the Kenyan coast.

Field Engineering Note
"The drums were pressure-tested, base-plated and anchor-bolted at the installation sites. Each unit is rated for a minimum 10-year coastal service life with annual inspection."

Total Bins Deployed Across the Kilifi Coastline
From repurposed industrial drums to purpose-donated units on World Environment Day, every bin placed on this coastline represents a permanent, community-owned node in a growing waste management network — built at zero cost to the public purse.
1
📍

Copacabana Beach — 20 Bins

The largest single deployment in the programme. Twenty bins were strategically positioned along the Copacabana Beach stretch — a high-traffic recreational and fishing zone — covering entry points, market stalls, and fish landing sites. Each unit was labelled, colour-coded, and anchored before handover to the local Beach Management Unit.

Copacabana · 20 Units
2
📍

Kuruwitu Marine Reserve — 15 Bins

Kuruwitu is home to Kenya's first community-managed marine conservancy — a model of grassroots ocean stewardship. Fifteen bins were deployed here in partnership with the Kuruwitu Conservation & Welfare Association, reinforcing the community's existing no-take zone commitment with the physical infrastructure to match. Every bin placed here is a vote of confidence in local governance.

Kuruwitu · 15 Units
3
🌍

World Environment Day — 20 Bins Donated

On World Environment Day this year, Project A.C.T. marked the occasion not with a press release — but with action. Twenty additional bins were donated to coastal communities as a public commitment to expanding the infrastructure network. This single-day event added a third wave of permanent waste stations to the coastline, bringing the total to 55 deployed units and signalling that the programme continues to grow.
