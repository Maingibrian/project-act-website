⬤ Community Initiative

On Thursday, March 26, 2026, Revital Healthcare (EPZ) Ltd, through its corporate social responsibility arm, Project A.C.T. (Action Changes Things), executed a targeted public health and environmental intervention at Vipingo Kichinjioni. Unlike previous coastline restoration efforts, this initiative was a rapid-response mobilization addressing a severe, localized bio-hazard crisis. Operating in a high-level coalition with the Mtwapa Municipality, the Department of Public Health (Kilifi South), and leading local environmental groups, Revital deployed a specialized strike team of 18 staff volunteers.

The operation yielded record-breaking results for the municipality. By neutralizing this immediate threat to groundwater and disease transmission, Revital Healthcare actively fulfilled its core corporate mandate: the preservation and protection of human life.

⬤ 2.0 Institutional Context

2.1 The Strategic Evolution of Project A.C.T. Since its inception, Project A.C.T. has focused on executing verifiable, high-impact community interventions. The Vipingo Kichinjioni operation marks a strategic evolution for the project — shifting from broad environmental stewardship toward precision-targeted public health risk mitigation. This aligns flawlessly with Revital Healthcare's identity as Africa's leading medical device manufacturer.

By deploying in-house PPE, clinical-grade containment protocols, and factory-trained personnel, the operation demonstrated that a corporate entity can function as an extension of the public health infrastructure — delivering outcomes that directly preserve human life at the community level.

⬤ 2.2 The Crisis

Kichinjioni:
The Hot-Spot.

The target location, Kichinjioni, was identified by municipal authorities as a critical hot-spot for the illegal dumping of sanitary waste. The accumulation of non-biodegradable, soiled diapers in open spaces and drainage channels created a severe bio-hazard, posing an imminent risk of groundwater contamination and the outbreak of diarrheal diseases such as cholera and typhoid among the local population.

⬤ 3.0 After-Action Review

Execution
on the
Ground.

The Vipingo Kichinjioni site presented a severe, localized public health crisis. Abandoned sanitary waste had accumulated in drainage ditches, creating breeding grounds for disease vectors and threatening groundwater integrity. Revital Healthcare A.C.T. mobilized an 18-person strike team within hours of the initial assessment.

"We treat the environment to treat the patient — before they ever reach the hospital."

On March 26th, the operation required grueling manual labor, extracting water-logged and heavily soiled sanitary waste from deep drainage ditches and dense brush. The integration of Revital staff with local CBO members ensured the workforce had both the protective gear required and the critical local geographical knowledge. Utilizing industrial-grade protective equipment and extraction tools, the team cleared four densely contaminated sectors in a single operational window.

⬤ 4.0 Logistics & Safety

Pre-Intervention
OHS Protocol.

Given the hazardous nature of the waste targeted for extraction, standard clean-up protocols were deemed insufficient. Strict Occupational Health and Safety (OHS) measures were prioritized. To safely handle biological waste, 18 Revital volunteers were equipped utilizing internal resources — gloves, masks, and containment sacks sourced directly from existing factory inventory and in-kind partnerships.

In-House Mobilization: All protective equipment was capitalized internally, resulting in KES 0 additional OHS cash cost — 100% internally capitalized.

⬤ 8.0 The Underlying Issue

Future Focus. As noted by the Public Health Office, the sheer volume of sanitary waste extracted — 7,800 soiled diapers — highlights a critical deficit in community education and sustainable waste management infrastructure. Future Project A.C.T. initiatives must pair physical clean-ups with sensitization campaigns and the development of lasting waste disposal solutions.

This operation solidified the trust between Revital Healthcare and the Department of Public Health, proving that corporate resources paired with civic duty is a powerful catalyst for meaningful, measurable change at the community level.
