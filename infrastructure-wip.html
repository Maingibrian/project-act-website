The Jikos
Zero-Waste Engineering

Converting Factory Waste
into Community Impact

Every piece of Project A.C.T. infrastructure originates from heavy industrial byproduct — raw steel chemical drums and spent refrigerant/gas cylinders destined for scrap yards. Instead of generating industrial waste, our engineering team intercepts these materials at source, upcycling factory trash into civic infrastructure that directly empowers local coastal towns.

These heavy-gauge steel vessels are thoroughly purged, stress-tested, welded shut, and treated with marine-grade anti-corrosive coatings. The result is robust, zero-cost public hardware built to withstand salt spray, intense heat, and high-volume daily community use for over a decade.
