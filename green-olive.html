1.0 Executive Summary

The Foundation
of Project A.C.T.

On December 10, 2025, Revital Healthcare (EPZ) Ltd. launched its corporate social responsibility arm, Project: Action Changes Things (A.C.T.), with a targeted social welfare outreach at Green Olive Children's Home in Kilifi County. A volunteer team of 14 employees delivered 88 individualized dignity packs, 110 plates of freshly prepared Pilau, and 88 school stationery sets — impacting every single child in the home.

Unlike generic donation drives, this mission was built on a Personal Dignity Strategy: every pack was colour‑coded by age and gender, 48 adolescent girls received a month's supply of sanitary products, and volunteers shared the meal seated among the children. The event proved that corporate empathy, when paired with rigorous resourcefulness, can produce transformational community outcomes.

2.0 Introduction & Strategic Context

Why Green Olive?
The Selection.

Green Olive Children's Home was identified through community networks as a high‑need facility caring for 88 orphaned and vulnerable children ranging from toddlers to teenagers. The home's director, Esther, confirmed critical gaps in hygiene supplies, school stationery, and balanced nutrition. The proximity to Revital Healthcare's headquarters allowed for streamlined logistics and direct volunteer engagement, maximizing time spent with the children.

Strategic Alignment: Phase 1 was deliberately chosen as a Social Welfare initiative, directly addressing human dignity and education — the foundational pillars upon which all subsequent Project A.C.T. phases were built.
Stakeholder Engagement

Pre-event coordination with Director Esther ensured every child's needs were assessed. Formal notifications were sent to the local area chief and community elders, guaranteeing a smooth, welcomed presence. Revital's internal A.C.T. committee also briefed all 14 volunteers on the Personal Dignity Strategy, emphasizing respect, warmth, and direct interaction.

3.0 Operational Design

88 Packs,
88 Stories.

The core innovation was the Personal Dignity Pack. Instead of bulk sacks, each child received a bag tailored to their age and gender. 48 adolescent girls were given a month's supply of sanitary pads, while every child received dental kits, soap, a face towel, and a full set of Kartasi school stationery. Volunteers personally handed each pack, calling the child by name.

The shared meal of 110 plates of Pilau transformed the visit into a celebration. Volunteers sat with the children, eating together, followed by impromptu games and storytelling. This unscripted connection proved to be the mission's emotional anchor.

Resource Mobilization

All supplies were sourced through internal corporate logistics and volunteer contributions. The operation demonstrated 100% resource efficiency — every item was accounted for and directly reached the children. The team leveraged existing supply chains to provide hygiene products, stationery, and fresh food without external fundraising.

⚡ Volunteer-Driven Impact: The entire initiative was powered by employee passion and corporate resource stewardship, proving that meaningful change doesn't require large budgets — just commitment and collaboration.
