Our Core Mandate

Health Begins
Outside the Clinic.

Revital Healthcare is dedicated to the preservation of human life through world-class medical manufacturing. However, true public health is not confined to clinical walls—it is inextricably linked to the ecosystems we inhabit.

This is why the vast majority of Project A.C.T.'s CSR operations directly target the environment. Environmental degradation is the root cause of countless public health crises. Whether we are clearing hazardous waste from the shores of Copacabana or restoring marine ecosystems at Kuruitu Beach, we are actively mitigating disease vectors at their source.

The Environmental Equation
Nature ⇄ Health
Every piece of plastic removed from our shores and every water source protected from contamination translates directly to a reduction in local disease. By treating the environment, we treat the patient before they ever reach the hospital.
Zero-Capital Sustainability

Built to
Outlast Us.

A cleanup treats a symptom; infrastructure cures the disease. To transition from a one-off corporate event to permanent community behavioral change, Revital Healthcare implemented a zero-capital sustainability model.

We upcycled 55 heavy-duty metal drums directly from the Revital factory floor. Welded, sanitized, and wrapped in high-visibility livery by our internal teams, these bins were installed at secure, monitored access points at zero cost to the project budget.

By implementing a strict, three-color segregation system, we empower residents to actively maintain the shoreline while feeding directly into the community's 'Trash for Cash' recycling economy.

Phase 06 · World Environment Day

5,000 Trees.
20 Bins.

On Friday, June 5, 2026, Project A.C.T. participated as a headline corporate partner in the official Kilifi County World Environment Day celebrations, hosted at Pwani University. The event brought together the County Government, NEMA, academia, and the private sector in a massive multi-stakeholder coalition.

"By planting 5,000 trees, we proved our willingness to do the hard, physical work of conservation. By donating 20 Legacy Bins, we proved our commitment to circular, sustainable infrastructure."

The Kilifi County Director of Environment publicly commended Project A.C.T. for its sustained, measurable impact — a milestone that cements Revital Healthcare's reputation as a premier corporate sustainability partner.
