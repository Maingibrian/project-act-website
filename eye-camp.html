01 — Mission Context

A Deliberate
Pivot to Direct
Medical Intervention.

Phase 05 April 18, 2026 Msumarini, Kikambala

Since its inception, Project A.C.T. has expanded its operational capacity with each phase — from social welfare at Green Olive Children's Home, to environmental mitigation at Kuruitu and Kichinjioni, to direct medical facilitation. Phase 5 marked a decisive strategic pivot: hosting a fully equipped ophthalmological clinic on Revital Healthcare's factory grounds.

Conducted on factory premises at Msumarini, the camp was engineered to absorb hundreds of elderly and visually impaired patients, routing them through a triage chain staffed by three distinct organizations working in unity under centralized corporate logistical command.

"By transitioning from environmental stewardship to direct clinical facilitation, Revital Healthcare successfully demonstrated its core mandate — the preservation and enhancement of human health."

As Africa's leading medical device manufacturer, hosting a specialist eye camp was not a departure from Revital's identity — it was the fullest expression of it.

02 — Operational Matrix

Operational Execution &
Coalition Dynamics.

2.1

The Partnership Matrix

The success of an event anticipating hundreds of patients required a robust, multi-agency model with clearly mapped jurisdictional roles. Lions Club of Mombasa Pwani served as Clinical Lead — providing specialized ophthalmologists, optometrists, diagnostic equipment, and pharmaceuticals. Revital Healthcare served as Venue & Logistics Host — providing secure grounds, infrastructure, crowd management, and hospitality. Pwani University School of Medicine supplied clinical support personnel, bridging specialist doctors and local patients through language and preliminary assessment.

⊕
3.0

Volunteer Integration & Triage

375 patients presented a significant logistical challenge, particularly given the predominantly elderly and visually impaired demographic. Strict crowd control and triage protocols were essential. Each Revital A.C.T. volunteer was paired with a Pwani University medical student — combining community authority with clinical knowledge.

3.2

Strategic Mentorship Model

This pairing was highly effective: Revital staff provided local community knowledge and crowd control authority, while medical students provided preliminary clinical understanding and accurate patient translation for the specialist team.

4.0

Pharmaceutical Reach

Hundreds of specialized ocular medications — including eye drops for infections — were donated and dispensed at the camp's pharmacy station, extending the clinical reach beyond diagnostics to active treatment.

03 — Chronology

Day of
Operations.

1
08:00 AM — Camp Opens
Gates Open · Triage Stations Activated

Patients begin arriving. Revital volunteers and Pwani University medical students man the gate registration and initial triage stations, sorting patients by urgency and condition.

2
08:30 AM — Clinical Operations Begin
Lions Club Specialists Begin Consultations

Ophthalmologists and optometrists from the Lions Club of Mombasa Pwani begin full diagnostic consultations. Visual acuity tests, cataract screenings, and prescription assessments run concurrently across multiple stations.

3
Ongoing — Pharmacy Dispersal
Eyewear & Medication Dispensed

Patients with prescriptions are directed to the dispensary station, where 91 pairs of corrective glasses are handed out alongside hundreds of units of specialized eye medications for ocular infections.

4
14:00 PM — Camp Conclusion
Final Patient Cleared · Target Met

The final patient cleared pharmacy triage exactly on target — completing a 6-hour, 375-patient clinical operation without compromising safety protocols. 10 high-risk cataract patients were identified and scheduled for fully funded corrective surgeries.

5
14:30 PM — Joint Meal
47 Volunteers Share a Catered Lunch

The operation concluded with a shared meal for all 47 field personnel — corporate staff, Lions Club volunteers, and Pwani University students — solidifying the professional bonds formed during a demanding day of collective service.
